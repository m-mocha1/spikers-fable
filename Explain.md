Hello, and thank you for the review.

We'd like to clarify how the membership status ("Active"/"Inactive") in our app works, as no in-app purchase is involved.

Spikers is a management app for an in-person volleyball club. Members attend physical, real-world training sessions held at a venue. Membership dues are collected entirely offline — in cash or by bank transfer, handled directly by the club's coaches outside of the app. The app does not process, collect, or facilitate any payment whatsoever. There is no checkout, no payment form, and no payment provider integrated.

The "Active/Inactive" indicator is simply a status flag that a coach toggles manually to reflect whether a member's offline dues are current. It is a record-keeping/attendance feature for a real-world service — comparable to a coach marking a roster.▎
Per App Store Review Guideline 3.1.3(e) and 3.1.5(a), goods and services consumed outside the app (here, in-person coaching) must use a purchase method other than in-app purchase, and in-app purchase is not permitted for them. Accordingly, our app correctly does not use IAP, because the only thing being paid for is a real-world, in-person service paid for offline.

To remove any ambiguity, in this build we have also updated the in-app wording to make the offline, real-world nature explicit (e.g., "Membership active/inactive — contact your coach," instead of "unlock sessions").

Please let us know if any further detail would help. Thank you.
